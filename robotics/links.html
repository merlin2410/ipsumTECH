Links of Robotic Manipulator

A robotic manipulator is a device that can perform various tasks by manipulating objects in its environment. A robotic manipulator consists of several rigid links connected by joints, which can be either fixed or movable. The links of a robotic manipulator are usually arranged in a serial or parallel configuration, depending on the desired degrees of freedom and workspace.

The links of a robotic manipulator are often modelled as vectors, which represent their position and orientation in a coordinate system. The vectors can be expressed in terms of joint variables, which are the angles or displacements of the joints. The relationship between the joint variables and the link vectors is called the kinematics of the robotic manipulator.

The kinematics of a robotic manipulator can be divided into two types: forward and inverse. Forward kinematics is the problem of finding the link vectors given the joint variables, while inverse kinematics is the problem of finding the joint variables given the link vectors. Forward kinematics is usually easier to solve than inverse kinematics, since there is a unique solution for each set of joint variables. However, inverse kinematics may have multiple or no solutions for a given set of link vectors, depending on the geometry and constraints of the robotic manipulator.

The links of a robotic manipulator are also subject to forces and torques, which affect their motion and stability. The forces and torques can be caused by gravity, friction, inertia, external loads, or actuation. The relationship between the forces and torques and the joint variables is called the dynamics of the robotic manipulator.

The dynamics of a robotic manipulator can be described by a set of differential equations, which relate the joint variables, their derivatives, and their second derivatives to the forces and torques. The differential equations can be derived using various methods, such as Lagrange's equations, Newton-Euler equations, or Kane's equations. The differential equations can be used to simulate the motion of the robotic manipulator, or to design controllers that can regulate its behaviour.

The links of a robotic manipulator are essential components that determine its performance and functionality. By understanding the kinematics and dynamics of the links, one can design and control robotic manipulators for various applications, such as industrial automation, medical surgery, space exploration, or entertainment.
