Open vs Closed Kinematic Chain

Open kinematic chains and closed kinematic chains are concepts used in robotics and mechanical engineering to describe the structure and movement of interconnected components, such as robotic arms or mechanical linkages. They differ in terms of how the components are connected and the resulting motion.

Open Kinematic Chain

  • In an open kinematic chain, there is a sequence of connected links or segments, where the last link or end effector is not connected to any other rigid body.
  • The open kinematic chain typically represents structures like robotic arms, human limbs, or industrial machines where the end-effector (such as a gripper or tool) can move freely in space.
  • The motion of the end effector is determined by the relative motions of the individual links in the chain.
  • Open kinematic chains offer flexibility in positioning and orientation but may require complex control algorithms to achieve precise movements.

Open kinematic chains are commonly used in a wide variety of robotic applications, such as:

  • Assembly
  • Manufacturing
  • Welding
  • Painting
  • Palletizing

Examples:

  • Robot Arms
  • Serial Manipulators
  • Scissor Lifts

Closed Kinematic Chain

  • In a closed kinematic chain, all the links or segments are connected in a loop, forming a closed structure.
  • The closed kinematic chain is often referred to as a mechanism or linkage, and it typically represents structures like bicycle linkages, car suspensions, or parallel robot manipulators.
  • In a closed kinematic chain, the motion of one link directly affects the motion of other links within the chain, making the movement of the end effector more predictable and constrained.
  • Closed kinematic chains are often used when precision and stability are essential, as the end effector's movement is limited by the design of the chain.

Closed kinematic chains are often used in applications where high stiffness and accuracy are required, such as:

  • Machine tools
  • Coordinate measuring machines (CMMs)
  • Surgical robots
  • Aerospace robots

Examples:

  • Parallel Manipulators
  • Hexapods
  • Stewart Platforms

In summary, the primary difference between open and closed kinematic chains lies in their structural arrangement and the consequences for motion. Open kinematic chains have one or more free-moving end effectors, offering more versatility but requiring sophisticated control. Closed kinematic chains have a fixed or more predictable relationship between the different links, making them suitable for applications where precision and stability are critical. Both concepts find applications in various fields, including robotics, mechanical engineering, and biomechanics, depending on the specific requirements of the task.
